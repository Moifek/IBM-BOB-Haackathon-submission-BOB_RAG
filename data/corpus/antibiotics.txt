Antibiotic Therapy: Principles and Common Agents

Antibiotics are medications used to treat bacterial infections. Appropriate antibiotic selection requires consideration of the suspected pathogen, site of infection, patient factors, and local resistance patterns.

Principles of Antibiotic Use:
1. Confirm or strongly suspect bacterial infection
2. Obtain cultures before initiating therapy when possible
3. Use narrow-spectrum agents when pathogen is known
4. Consider local antibiogram and resistance patterns
5. Ensure adequate dosing and duration
6. Monitor for adverse effects and therapeutic response

Common Antibiotic Classes:

Beta-Lactams:
- Penicillins (amoxicillin, ampicillin)
- Cephalosporins (cephalexin, ceftriaxone)
- Carbapenems (meropenem, imipenem)
- Mechanism: Inhibit bacterial cell wall synthesis

Fluoroquinolones:
- Ciprofloxacin, levofloxacin, moxifloxacin
- Mechanism: Inhibit DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV
- Black box warnings: Tendon rupture, peripheral neuropathy, aortic dissection

Macrolides:
- Azithromycin, clarithromycin, erythromycin
- Mechanism: Inhibit bacterial protein synthesis (50S ribosomal subunit)
- Common uses: Respiratory tract infections, atypical pneumonia

Tetracyclines:
- Doxycycline, minocycline
- Mechanism: Inhibit bacterial protein synthesis (30S ribosomal subunit)
- Contraindicated in pregnancy and children <8 years

Aminoglycosides:
- Gentamicin, tobramycin, amikacin
- Mechanism: Inhibit protein synthesis
- Toxicities: Nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity (requires monitoring)

Common Infections and Empiric Therapy:

Community-Acquired Pneumonia:
- Outpatient: Amoxicillin or doxycycline
- Inpatient: Ceftriaxone + azithromycin

Urinary Tract Infection:
- Uncomplicated: Nitrofurantoin or trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole
- Complicated: Fluoroquinolone or ceftriaxone

Skin and Soft Tissue Infection:
- Mild: Cephalexin or dicloxacillin
- MRSA suspected: Doxycycline or trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole

Antibiotic Resistance:
Major concern worldwide. Key resistant organisms include:
- Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA)
- Extended-spectrum beta-lactamase (ESBL) producing Enterobacteriaceae
- Carbapenem-resistant Enterobacteriaceae (CRE)
- Vancomycin-resistant Enterococcus (VRE)

Antibiotic Stewardship:
Programs designed to optimize antibiotic use, improve patient outcomes, reduce resistance, and decrease healthcare costs. Key strategies include prospective audit and feedback, formulary restrictions, and education.
